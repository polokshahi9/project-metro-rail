Dhaka Metro Rail is a rapid transit system that is currently under construction in the capital city of Bangladesh, Dhaka. It will be the first metro rail system in Bangladesh, and is expected to provide a much-needed transportation solution for the growing population of the city. The project is being implemented by the Dhaka Mass Transit Company Limited (DMTCL), with technical assistance from the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) and financial assistance from the Asian Development Bank (ADB). The metro rail system will have a total length of 20.1 km and 16 stations, with both elevated and underground sections. The first phase of the project, which includes the construction of a 10.6 km elevated section with 11 stations, is expected to be completed by 2022. The Dhaka Metro Rail system is expected to alleviate the severe traffic congestion in the city, reduce travel time, and provide a comfortable and safe mode of transportation for commuters. It is also expected to have a positive impact on the city's economy and create job opportunities.
